# QA / Executive Deck / Regulated

## Instant fail

FAIL immediately if the output:
- sells a platform instead of `genu.mark`;
- shows `ERP` as part of the core;
- shows `еАкциз` as part of the core;
- mentions `genu.code` in the early regulated story;
- mixes regulated and voluntary logic;
- sounds like internal draft notes or operator-source copy;
- omits a next step to audit / architecture session / pilot.
- introduces a new unsourced product, architecture layer, or platform explanation;
- uses weak superiority claims without explicit proof.

## Asset checklist

- The output is an executive deck that is usually 10-12 slides.
- 8-9 slides are acceptable only if no mandatory meaning is lost.
- 13-14 slides are acceptable only if each extra slide closes a distinct buying-question.
- The narrative order stays product-first and anxiety-reducing.
- The ERP split is explicit in the final wording.
- `еАкциз` remains external.
- The output is modern Ukrainian and buyer-facing.

## Asset-specific acceptance criteria

- The deck opens with inevitable `еАкциз` launch.
- The first screen and first paragraph clearly sell `genu.mark` as the safe path to `еАкциз` implementation.
- The deck frames the buyer problem as safe implementation without production risk.
- The deck shows `genu.mark` as the line core.
- The deck makes the simple strong-core logic easy to retell.
- The deck shows `ERP` and `еАкциз` as external contours.
- The deck explains why strong core first lowers rollout risk.
- The deck makes interface and exception risk visible.
- The deck positions `Dominanta + Logictime` as the safer path.
- The deck ends with audit / architecture session / pilot.

## Final pass question

Would an executive buyer understand what is sold, what is core, what is external, why the launch path is safer, and what to do next without oral explanation?
