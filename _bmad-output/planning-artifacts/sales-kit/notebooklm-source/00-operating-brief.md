# Notebook LM Operating Brief

## Main rule

Notebook LM does not invent strategy.

For mandatory regulated assets it must work only from:
- `regulated-final-bundle/01-control-packet.md`;
- `regulated-final-bundle/02-approved-regulated-source-packet.md`;
- one approved buyer-facing regulated asset;
- one matching brief.

## One notebook = one asset

Do not mix in one notebook:
- more than one mandatory regulated asset;
- voluntary materials;
- optional shared materials unless the brief explicitly requires them;
- website or product strategy docs.

## Hard exclusions

- do not sell platform over product;
- do not make `ERP` or `еАкциз` look like the core;
- do not lead with `genu.im`;
- do not introduce `genu.code` into early regulated narrative;
- do not output operator-language or meta-language.

## Output contract

Notebook LM must produce:
- modern Ukrainian;
- self-contained buyer-facing material;
- one clear main message per slide / block;
- explicit CTA to audit / architecture session / pilot;
- no unsupported claims.

## Post-generation check

Reject immediately if the answer to any question is `no`:
- Is it obvious that `genu.mark` is being sold?
- Is the core separate from external contours?
- Is the ERP split shown exactly?
- Is `еАкциз` clearly external?
- Is early `genu.code` absent?
- Does it move to audit / architecture session / pilot?
