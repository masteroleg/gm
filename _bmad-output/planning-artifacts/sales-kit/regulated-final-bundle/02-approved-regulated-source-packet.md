## Simple core formula

Every regulated asset may phrase it differently, but the buyer must quickly understand that `genu.mark`:
- receives codes;

- aggregates;
- gives packaging result.

## Architecture that must remain identical everywhere

### Core

`genu.mark` is the production-line core.

Inside the core live:
- line flow;
- mark application;
- verification;
- reject / rework control;
- aggregation;
- line result state.

### External contours

Outside the core live three separate contours:
- `ERP -> source of production job`;
- `ERP / internal systems <- exchange of results and statuses`;
- `–µ–ê–∫—Ü–∏–∑` as the separate external state contour.

## What the buyer is buying

The buyer is not buying abstract ecosystem language.

The buyer is buying:
- a controllable line core;
- clear external handoffs;
- safer rollout discipline;
- reviewable evidence and responsibilities.

## Why Dominanta + Logictime

- `Dominanta` brings Domino equipment and trusted commercial entry.
- `Logictime` owns architecture, integration, exceptions, launch, and rollout discipline across `genu.*`.
- Together they reduce the chance that the project becomes a patchwork of equipment, manual workarounds, and unclear responsibility.

## Exception and evidence rules

Every regulated asset may speak differently, but all must preserve these ideas:
- real risk lives on interfaces, exceptions, and recovery;
- offline, reject, rework, and aggregation are not secondary topics;
- evidence must be designed in from the start;
- responsibilities must be visible before rollout.
