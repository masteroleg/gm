# Regulated Buyer Model

## Core thesis

The first regulated buyer is not shopping for novelty.

They are trying to avoid:
- line chaos;
- rollout failure;
- broken interfaces;
- unclear responsibilities;
- weak evidence during review.

## What they buy first

They buy:
- safe implementation;
- operational stability;
- a controllable core on the line;
- clean handoffs to external contours;
- evidence they can defend internally.

## What they do not buy first

They do not buy first for:
- brand storytelling;
- ecosystem breadth;
- optional future upside;
- feature theater.

## Buying committee

### Economic sponsor

Primary question:
- Is this the safest way to get mandatory implementation done?

### Operational owner

Primary question:
- Will the line stay controllable when exceptions happen?

### Technical owner

Primary question:
- Is the core clear, and are external contours separated cleanly?

### Risk / audit / procurement reviewer

Primary question:
- Are the claims, boundaries, and evidence reviewable?

## Buyer language

Best language:
- safe rollout;
- implementation confidence;
- strong core first;
- clean interfaces;
- evidence;
- calmer launch.

Weak first-contact language:
- ecosystem;
- transformation;
- marketing upside;
- innovation theater.

## CTA ladder

- early = audit / working session;
- early-mid = architecture session;
- mid = exception review / evidence review;
- mid-late = pilot map;
- late = rollout plan.
