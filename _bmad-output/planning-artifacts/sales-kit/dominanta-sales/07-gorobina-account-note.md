# Gorobina Account Note

## Purpose

This note is account-specific.

It exists to help the next Gorobina conversation:
- stay inside the `genu.mark`-first regulated strategy;
- respond to competitive pressure without panic;
- move from theory to a concrete pilot / contour-review next step.

It must not be reused as generic buyer-facing copy.

## 1. Account reality seen in the meeting

### What the buyer already knows

- the plant is already exposed to `еАкциз` requirements;
- they have already seen equipment discussion before;
- they are not at zero-awareness stage;
- they are not asking for general education only.

### What the buyer still lacks

- a concrete picture of what to test first;
- a low-overhead path to start learning on their real contour;
- confidence that the project will not stay at theory level;
- a clear next commercial and technical step.

## 2. Stakeholder picture

The meeting included multiple buyer-side roles at once:

- director / sponsor presence;
- engineering and operations signals;
- legal / accounting sensitivity in the background;
- practical plant-side questions about what can be tried now.

Implication:

- this is not a one-person technical evaluation;
- the next step must be simple enough to explain upward and practical enough to discuss with the plant.

## 3. Competitive pressure

### What happened

- a competing solution-side actor was present in the room;
- that actor was not only pushing software, but also moving into hardware territory;
- this threatens both Logictime and Dominanta if the account is left inside a vague “let's compare vendors” frame.

### What this means

If Dominanta stays only in hardware language, the competitor can try to own the “whole answer”.

The response is not to widen the story into platform-first language.

The response is:

- Dominanta remains the trusted equipment and commercial entry;
- `genu.mark` remains the strong core being sold;
- Logictime owns contour design, interfaces, exceptions, and launch path;
- the next step becomes a concrete pilot / contour review, not another abstract presentation.

## 4. Strong buyer signals from the transcript

### Signal A. The buyer wants something more concrete than theory

The account explicitly asked, in effect:
- what can be shown;
- what can be tried;
- what can be priced next.

Interpretation:

- more abstract theory will weaken the position;
- the next meeting should produce a visible plan or pilot shape.

### Signal B. A “minimum program” is commercially attractive

There was direct interest in a minimal scenario that allows the customer to:
- touch the process;
- try it on something real;
- learn the software and physical flow;
- delay heavy rollout decisions until the contour is clearer.

Interpretation:

- a pilot-first motion is not just technically correct;
- it is commercially attractive for this account.

### Signal C. Line photos and physical constraints matter early

The discussion naturally moved toward:
- sending line information;
- reviewing photos;
- checking whether there is physical access and fit.

Interpretation:

- the next step should request concrete line materials, not another general meeting;
- this account will respond better to line-fit discussion than to abstract product talk.

### Signal D. The buyer is open to staged learning

The meeting showed willingness to:
- try a smaller setup;
- learn on a limited scope;
- explore manual or simplified pilot mechanics before full line rollout.

Interpretation:

- use this to support a calm pilot narrative;
- do not force a full-contour sell too early.

## 5. Recommended account frame

Use this exact logic in follow-up:

1. `еАкциз` rollout is coming.
2. The real question is how to start without line chaos.
3. `genu.mark` is the strong line core.
4. Dominanta gives the right Domino entry on the equipment side.
5. Logictime maps the real contour, interfaces, exceptions, and launch path.
6. The safest next move is a practical pilot / contour review, not a debate about every future integration.

## 6. What to emphasize next time

### Emphasize

- not just hardware, but a workable launch path;
- concrete first step instead of a giant all-at-once project;
- the idea of starting with the core and adding external blocks deliberately;
- the fact that physical line fit and launch discipline matter;
- the fact that pilot learning reduces risk before wider rollout.

### De-emphasize

- abstract SaaS language;
- ecosystem-first or platform-first framing;
- generalized regulation explainer mode;
- early commercial complexity before the contour is understood;
- broad “we do everything” positioning.

## 7. What not to say

- do not attack the competitor emotionally;
- do not promise full compliance or guaranteed acceptance;
- do not let the customer reduce the project to “just a printer”;
- do not let the conversation stay only in “software subscription” language;
- do not let `genu.im` become the opening object of sale;
- do not let `genu.code` enter the account narrative.

## 8. Best next-step proposal for this account

### Primary recommendation

Offer a two-part next step:

1. a compact contour review;
2. a pilot-definition pass.

### What this should include

- current line overview;
- photos or video of the line and access constraints;
- current print / apply or label mechanics;
- packaging hierarchy and aggregation expectations;
- where the first production job enters;
- what level of pilot is acceptable to the customer;
- what can be tried without full rollout overhead.

### Why this is the right move

It answers the buyer's real signals:
- “show something concrete”;
- “give us a minimum workable start”;
- “help us understand fit on our real line”.

## 9. Commercial shape hinted by the meeting

The meeting suggests the account may respond well to a commercial structure with:

- a defined one-time contour / implementation / training layer;
- then a clearer operating model after the contour is understood.

But guardrail:

- do not lead the next conversation with pricing mechanics alone;
- first anchor the account on the right pilot and contour scope.

## 10. Pilot hypothesis

The strongest pilot hypothesis from the meeting is:

- start with a minimal, learnable scenario;
- prove the flow on a real but limited contour;
- use that to remove uncertainty around physical fit, operator flow, and launch logic;
- then scale toward fuller rollout.

This fits the regulated strategy because it still leads to:
- audit;
- architecture session;
- pilot.

## 11. Information to collect before the next round

- panorama photos or short video of the line;
- bottle / label geometry and current application mechanics;
- available install space and access restrictions;
- target line speed;
- packaging hierarchy requirements;
- current and expected aggregation scenarios;
- planned ERP touchpoints;
- desired pilot scope;
- who must approve the next step on the buyer side.

## 12. Internal talk-track for Dominanta

Safe short framing:

`Почнемо не з великої теорії і не з випадкового набору блоків. Давайте швидко розкладемо ваш реальний контур, покажемо сильне ядро genu.mark і визначимо мінімальний пілот, який дасть вам практичну ясність без зайвого навантаження на старті.`

## 13. Hardware and visual notes

Potential later visual references for this account:

- Domino equipment on the application side;
- Omron scanners on verification / scan points;
- Advantech industrial touch PCs for operator interaction;
- a simple line-fit diagram rather than a software architecture poster.

Guardrail:

- these visuals should support the contour story;
- they must not replace the `genu.mark`-first message.

## Bottom line

For Gorobina, the best move is not to escalate abstraction.

The best move is:

- keep Dominanta strong at entry;
- keep `genu.mark` as the thing being sold;
- use Logictime to turn the discussion into a real contour review;
- propose a minimum practical pilot;
- move the account out of theory and out of competitor-led confusion.
