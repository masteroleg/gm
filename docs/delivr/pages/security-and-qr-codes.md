QR codes have emerged as a strategic asset for modern businesses — delivering seamless access to digital experiences, capturing engagement in real time, and connecting audiences to the brands they love. But as adoption grows, so do security risks — from malicious redirects to data theft and malware. For businesses, protecting digital touchpoints is no longer optional. A security-focused QR code management platform, like Delivr, is a reliable solution that helps mitigate these threats and provides complete control over how codes are created, deployed, and tracked — ensuring every scan is safe, secure, and on-brand.

Prevent Unauthorized Access
---------------------------

Delivr’s security features, such as two-factor authentication (2FA) and secure Single Sign-On (SSO), are designed to prevent unauthorized account access effectively. This ensures that QR codes remain controlled by legitimate organizations, making it significantly harder for cybercriminals to compromise accounts, even if login credentials are stolen or otherwise compromised.

Furthermore, Delivr has measures that prevent brute-force attacks, allow for configurable session lengths, send email notifications of logins from new IPs/devices, provide the ability to sign out of all other devices and web sessions, and more.

Dynamic QR Codes
----------------

Dynamic QR codes allow for updates or deactivation after distribution. Organizations can modify the destination URL in real time without reprinting codes if unusual scanning activity is detected or a QR code is flagged as suspicious. This adaptability reassures organizations that they can respond to changing security needs, making it harder for attackers to exploit QR codes and giving you the power to control your digital interactions.

Compliance with Security Standards
----------------------------------

Adhering to security regulations, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), ensures QR code security, particularly when handling sensitive user data. Compliance builds consumer trust and reinforces a company’s commitment to protecting digital interactions.
