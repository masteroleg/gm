QR Codes for Public Spaces.
===========================

QR codes are increasingly being used in public spaces to share information, preserve art, and create interactive experiences. From parks and monuments to murals and installations, a simple scan can connect people to stories, history, and digital content that would otherwise be impossible to display physically.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Public art, in particular, has found creative uses for QR codes.

Using QR Codes to Restore Murals to Their Original State (via Wooster Collective).
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A powerful example comes from a mural featured by *Wooster Collective* in Vancouver, British Columbia. At some point, the mural was vandalized with graffiti. Instead of simply repainting over the damage, a QR code was painted over the offending tag.

When scanned, the QR code directed viewers to an image of the mural before it was vandalized — effectively restoring the artwork digitally.

This creative approach allowed the public to see the original piece as the artist intended, while also making a statement about preservation and the evolving nature of street art. It turned an act of vandalism into an opportunity for digital storytelling.

How QR Codes Add Value in Public Spaces.
----------------------------------------

This example shows how QR codes can go beyond marketing and serve cultural and educational purposes. In public spaces, QR codes can be used to:

*   Share the history of murals, monuments, or landmarks
*   Display “before and after” restoration images
*   Provide artist biographies and project details
*   Link to audio guides or video explanations
*   Offer multilingual information for tourists

Because the linked content can be updated anytime, cities and artists can keep information current without altering the physical space again.

Blending Physical and Digital Experiences.
------------------------------------------

QR codes help bridge the gap between the physical and digital worlds. In public spaces, they allow limited physical surfaces to connect to unlimited digital content. Whether preserving artwork, educating visitors, or enhancing cultural engagement, QR codes provide a simple yet impactful solution.

By thoughtfully integrating QR codes into murals and public installations, communities can protect artistic intent, encourage interaction, and make public spaces more informative and engaging.
